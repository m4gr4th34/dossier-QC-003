## Arc P — THE PLATFORM ARC (reframe; supersedes E/A/D as standalone arcs)

*"Which physical platform reaches fault-tolerant QC first — explained so a high
school student can follow every engineering fact, graded honestly on one ruler,
with dated public bets."*

Three layers, each mapping to a surviving Cycle-1 component:

1. **The physical layer, surveyed honestly** (new; the author's Pull). What each
   platform is physically made of, what binds it, its verified error rates and
   demonstrated results, vendor claims labeled as vendor claims. Platforms in
   scope: neutral atoms, superconducting, trapped ions, photonics, cat/bosonic
   qubits, spin qubits. Bar set by the author: every engineering fact explained at
   high-school level — "we've already written a better 'state of the field' than
   anything that exists out there, including actual books." Achievable because
   books cannot carry per-claim honest labels, dated falsifiable bets, or updates;
   this format can.
2. **The race, recosted** (absorbs arc E). Each platform's flagship quantitative
   claim (250x, 752x, 138x, 20-40x, 10x, 8x — see ledger arc E table) re-expressed
   on one common ruler. Exhibit 0 and the SR errata stand as the arc's founding
   demonstration.
3. **Dated public bets** (absorbs arc D). Registered forecasts on platform
   milestones with mechanical resolution. Percentages: author authority.

Plus the author's exploratory extension, doctrine-bound:
4. **Poking the facts.** For each binding engineering constraint, ask: is a novel
   solution or a new platform configuration visible? **Publishing wall:** any such
   idea ships only as a SCOUTED CLAIM (derivation, simulation with artifacts, or
   dated falsifiable forecast). Bare idea lists are inadmissible per the founding
   handoff §5.

Honesty constraint, carried from the decision session: the platform race partly
turns on engineering facts no simulation settles (laser power scaling, fabrication
yield, cryogenic plant economics). The arc grades published evidence and registers
dated probabilities; it does not derive a winner from first principles, and says so
in print.

Verification rule for Layer 1, standing: every engineering fact is search-verified
on a stated date before it is asserted; recalled numbers are inadmissible. The
CITE/REPORTED split applies inside single sentences (measured result vs vendor
roadmap).
